# Monetization & Go-To-Market Plan

## Monetization Model

**Primary: Commission per successful placement OR flat per-post fee, charged to providers
only.**

- Students pay nothing — they are highly price-sensitive, and charging them would
  suppress adoption on the side of the marketplace that's already harder to acquire.
- Two pricing options to test in Phase 1:
  1. **Flat per-post fee** (e.g. a small fixed amount per job listing) — simpler to
     understand, predictable for providers, easier to implement (no need to track
     "successful placement" for billing).
  2. **Commission per successful placement** (e.g. a percentage or flat amount once a
     job is marked complete) — aligns price with value delivered, but requires reliable
     "job completed" tracking to bill correctly.
- Recommendation: start with a **flat per-post fee**, or even **free posting during the
  Phase 0/Phase 1 pilot** to remove all friction while validating the model, then
  introduce pricing once provider demand is proven.

## Future Revenue Lines (Phase 2/3, not MVP)
- Featured/boosted job posts (shown higher in the feed) — easy to add once there's enough
  job volume that placement matters.
- Premium provider tools (analytics, repeat-hire suggestions) as a subscription.
- Small fee on the instant UPI payout feature once built (Phase 3) — common pattern for
  instant-payout features elsewhere.

## Go-To-Market Plan

### Stage 1 — Single Campus/Area Pilot (Phase 0 + Phase 1)
- Pick one campus cluster or local market area with a real concentration of small
  businesses (cafes, retail, event organizers) and students.
- Manually recruit the first 15-20 providers in person/via WhatsApp — this also doubles
  as direct manual verification, solving the trust/scam problem for v1 without needing
  automated checks yet.
- Recruit students via campus channels — student WhatsApp groups, college notice boards,
  word of mouth, a few campus ambassadors (often students themselves, sometimes paid in
  free perks or small commission for referrals).

### Stage 2 — Prove Retention, Expand Within the Same City
- Once the pilot area shows healthy repeat usage (providers posting a second job,
  students completing more than one job), expand to additional campuses/areas within the
  same city before going to a new city.
- Use real trust-score and completed-job data as marketing proof points ("X jobs
  completed, Y verified providers") to recruit the next wave faster.

### Stage 3 — Multi-City Expansion (Phase 3)
- Only after the model is proven in one city with healthy unit economics (cost to acquire
  a provider/student vs. revenue per provider).
- Consider city-by-city rollout rather than a simultaneous multi-city launch, to keep
  manual verification and quality control manageable as long as it remains a manual
  process.

## Competitive Positioning (for marketing messaging)
- Against Apna/WorkIndia: "Built only for short, hyperlocal student gigs — not full-time
  jobs you don't have time for."
- Against Internshala: "Real paid work you can do this week, not just internships."
- Core promise to lead with: **fast, safe, nearby, short-duration jobs — with a trust
  score and buddy-apply that make it safer than other platforms.**
